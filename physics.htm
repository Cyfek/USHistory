History

Fission was discovered in 1938 by the German physicist Otto Hahn, and the theory behind it was worked out by Lise Meitner the next year. However, it would take 6 more years before it could be harnessed for use in a bomb.

H-Bombs

An H-Bomb, or "Thermonuclear Bomb" is a more powerful atomic weapon, based on fusion, instead of fission. It has two parts: A Primary, which is itself an A-bomb, and a Secondary, which contains light elements (that is, elements with small nuclei).
