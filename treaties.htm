Nuclear Treaties

Over time, a number of treaties were signed betwewen the US, USSR, and other nuclear states, to stop or slow the testing of nuclear weapons. The following is a list of the few of the most important. (TBT = Test Ban Treaty)



1963 - Limited TBT - The first such treaty; it meant that tests had to be carried out underground, to reduce the risk of fall-out harming civilians.

1974 - Threshold TBT - Meant that tests were limited to an explosive power of 150 kilotons of TNT.

1976 - Peaceful TBT - Made an agreement for outside observers to check for compliance with the above treaties.


1970 - Non-Proliferation Treaty - Provides for a peaceful expasion of nuclear tech, while limiting the ability of nations to become new nuclear states.
